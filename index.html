Selections from CWD Index

100,000 Stars (Michael Chang, Jono Brandel, Valdean Klump, et al, 2012)

a interactive map of all the nearby stars based on real astronomical datasets. allowing you to zoom, pan, and explore space right in your browser!
this website is very dear to me because I remember going on it around 2014 and thinking it was the coolest website ever made (i was nine), but regardless it is a remarkable showcase on how technology had evolved around that time and now it is a reminder how how much we have evolved since then.

Here is Today (No info on the CWD)

a creative web project that illustrates the entire span of the universe’s history, showing how tiny “today” is in that vast context, giving users a sense of deep time by letting you scroll or interact with the timeline visually, which is definitely something difficult to grasp with static timelines.
this is also a website i visted around high school if i remember correctly, and it definitely was a cool way visualize such a large amount of time.

thisxdoesnotexist (Kashish Hora, 2019)

a directory showcasing examples of generative AI at work, from faces to objects that don’t exist, all produced by neural networks and GAN models.
this website is completely new to me unlike the other two and i believe has a new context under today's climate compared to when it was created. generative AI has evolved to a scary point where it can now fool the average person not attuned to it, and this website has definitely become a documentation of how generative AI has evolved since its development.
